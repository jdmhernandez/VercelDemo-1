One Piece TCG — Starter Guide

Quick setup, deck building rules, sample decks & play tips to get you playing fast.

1) Quick Start — how a game begins

This is a short walkthrough to let you start a friendly match ASAP.

  1. Choose a Leader: Your Leader determines the color(s) your deck can use.
  2. Build a 50-card deck: Choose only cards that match your Leader's color(s). Leader cards may have dual colors — if so you can include both colors.
  3. Shuffle & Draw: Draw 7 cards. If you don't like your hand, you may mulligan by shuffling back and drawing 6, 5, ... down to 1.
  4. Decide First Player: Use dice/coin to decide who goes first.
  5. Play: Follow the turn structure (Draw, Main, Attack, End).
Leader color rule (important):
You may only include cards that share a color with your Leader. Example: Red Leader → only Red cards; Red/Green Leader → Red and Green cards allowed.

2) Building a deck — practical tips

  • Stick to a clear plan: rush/aggression (damage early), midrange/control (value trades), or combo.
  • Count your energy/cost curve: include lower cost cards for early pressure and a few higher cost finishers.
  • Include draw/search effects and a few removal or bounce effects to handle threats.
  • Test and tune: swap weak cards after games — a good starter deck should be tuned by 5–10 changes.

3) Sample starter decks (copy and adapt)

These are small, friendly starter lists — not tournament lists. Use them as a template.

How to use these lists:

  • Start with a 50-card base: you can copy these and add/remove similar cards.
  • Practice mulligans to improve your starting hand reliability.
  • Record wins/losses after adjustments so you can see what works.

4) Turn phases (simple)

  1. Draw Phase: Draw 1 card (first player may skip this on turn 1 depending on rules).
  2. Main Phase: Play characters, events, activate abilities.
  3. Attack Phase: Declare attacks with characters. Attacks can damage opposing characters or Leader (depending on ability/type).
  4. End Phase: Resolve end-of-turn triggers, opponent readies their turn.

(This is a simplified overview — check the rulebook for precise timing windows and keyword interactions.)

5) Practical tips that matter

  • Play tight early: If your deck is aggressive, take initiative and force bad trades.
  • Value trades: If playing midrange/control, trade efficiently — lose less value than you give.
  • Know your Leader: Leaders define your strategy — build around them, not the other way round.
  • Mulligan smart: Don’t keep a useless hand just because you fear fewer cards; a good hand is better than more bad cards.

6) FAQ

Q: Can I play cards of other colors?

Only if your Leader allows those colors. Example: Dual-color Leader lets you use both its colors.

Q: How many copies of a card?

Standard limit is up to 4 copies of a non-unique card, but check the latest official banlist or rules — starter decks may have specific limits.

Q: Where to practice?

Local game stores, online communities, or tabletop nights are great. Playing lots improves your deck intuition the fastest.
